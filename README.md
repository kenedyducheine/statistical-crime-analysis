# Statistical Crime Analysis
For this project, we will be analyzing the results of research paper that looks at the hidden cost of using Hawkes processes over log Gaussian to analyze crime. 

The researchers use two different mathematical models to explain the clustering of crime. The Hawkes Point Process is a stochastic process in which the occurrence of one event triggers and increases the probability of future events. This creates a "chain reaction" of crime clustering, and is the current assumption regarding crime clustering. Alternatively, the Log-Gaussian Cox Process is a double stochastic process that analyzes the spatial aggregation of the clustering in order to determine if it is due to underlying factors (socioeconomic status, access to education / jobs, etc).

Our investigation will analyze crime data from Chicago and Vancouver, Canada. We will perform our own regressions and least squares approximations to determine if the researcher's claim about crime clustering is accurate.

Our research could be used to further analyze modern policing practices and help allocate resources to high crime areas.
